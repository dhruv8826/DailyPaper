Here are five Indian stocks that appear undervalued based on their Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratios and insights from recent financial filings:

**Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)** Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) presents as an undervalued stock with a trailing P/E ratio currently ranging from approximately 9.95x to 11.1x as of April 2026. This relatively low P/E is supported by strong recent financial performance, including a significant increase in Profit after Tax for 9MFY23 and the highest-ever Half Yearly Profit After Tax for H1FY24. The company also demonstrated a robust 17.2% year-on-year growth in Quarterly Net Profit for December 2025, alongside strong growth in total premium income, suggesting a healthy and expanding business that the current valuation may not fully reflect.

**Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Ltd (ONGC)** Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Ltd (ONGC) exhibits characteristics of an undervalued stock, with its trailing P/E ratio around 9.42x to 9.5x and a forward P/E of 8.38x. Recent earnings reported in February 2026 significantly surpassed analyst expectations, with Earnings Per Share (EPS) showing a positive surprise of 36.5% and revenue exceeding forecasts by 430.7%. Furthermore, the company's Quarterly Net Profit for December 2025 recorded a 1.6% year-on-year growth, indicating consistent profitability that, coupled with its low P/E, suggests the market may be underpricing its future potential.

**ICICI Bank** ICICI Bank, one of India's leading private sector banks, appears undervalued with a trailing P/E of approximately 18x as of April 2026. While not a single-digit P/E, recent analyses highlight a "valuation de-rating" and the stock trading meaningfully below its annual peak, suggesting market undervaluing despite robust financial health. Recent filings reveal a 7.1% increase in net profit for Q4FY26 and a 7.7% year-on-year core operating profit growth in FY26, showcasing solid operational performance and growth that could justify a higher valuation.

**State Bank of India (SBI)** State Bank of India (SBI), India's largest public sector bank, currently holds a P/E ratio in the range of 12.04x to 12.85x (TTM as of December 2025 to April 2026). Although some reports note its P/E might be at a premium to the public sector banking industry average, its P/E is also near its historical median, indicating it may be undervalued relative to its own consistent performance and growth trajectory. The bank has demonstrated strong long-term performance and consistent earnings growth, with its EPS for the trailing twelve months ending December 2025 reported at ₹91.62, suggesting an attractive valuation for long-term investors.

**NTPC Ltd** NTPC Ltd, a major power generation company, presents a P/E ratio ranging from approximately 14.80x (FY25) to 15.8x (latest TTM). While this P/E is noted to be greater than its five-year average, it could still be considered reasonable for a company of its scale and stability in the power sector, with some analyses still categorizing it with a "Very Good PE." The company's recent filings indicate a positive trend, with a 7.11% growth in net profit for December 2025, demonstrating healthy financial performance that might not be fully reflected in its current market price.
