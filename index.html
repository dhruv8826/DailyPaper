Front Page

  • 1. The United States canceled a diplomatic mission to Pakistan aimed at holding peace talks with Iran, following the departure of senior Iranian officials from Islamabad and continued tensions over the Strait of Hormuz. US President Trump had ordered a naval blockade of Iranian ports in response to Iran's closure of the vital shipping route, and the two nations remain at an impasse regarding a lasting ceasefire in the ongoing conflict.
  • 2. A temporary ceasefire in Lebanon has provided some relief, but a significant displacement crisis persists as civilians struggle to return to their homes amidst the continued instability in the region. International mediators are actively working to stabilize the situation, though violations and deep-seated mistrust continue to challenge peace efforts.
  • 3. Mali is experiencing a surge in coordinated attacks across the country, including in its capital Bamako, carried out by various armed groups. Tuareg rebels have reportedly seized control of Kidal in northern Mali after overcoming government forces, while other groups, including Al-Qaeda-linked JNIM, have claimed responsibility for the widespread assaults.
  • 4. Russian forces launched overnight drone and ballistic missile attacks on Dnipro, Ukraine, resulting in the deaths of eight people and injuring 49 others. This latest strike highlights the ongoing aerial assaults affecting Ukrainian cities.
  • 5. Hungary is currently facing significant political developments with intensifying elections that could potentially mark a historic turning point, signaling the end of a 16-year era under Viktor Orbán and ushering in an uncertain new chapter for the country's domestic and foreign policy direction.
  • 6. Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez raised concerns during talks in Beijing about Europe's growing and unsustainable trade deficit with China, urging Beijing to open its markets to European goods. Spain alone recorded a substantial trade deficit with China, and Sanchez warned that this imbalance grew significantly in the past year.
  • 7. The United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA) has issued a warning that the continent risks remaining uncompetitive globally if it does not swiftly adopt data and frontier technologies like Artificial Intelligence. A senior official stressed the urgency for African nations to harness AI to drive economic growth and ensure competitiveness in the digital economy.
  • 8. New estimates from the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) indicate that the ongoing military escalation in the Middle East, now in its fifth week, is projected to cost economies in the region between 3.7% and 6.0% of their collective Gross Domestic Product (GDP). This escalation has reversed more than a year of economic growth in the affected areas.
  • 9. At least seven people were killed and 17 others injured in a bombing incident on the Pan-American Highway in the Cauca Department of Colombia. The attack adds to the security challenges faced in the region.
  • 10. Nizar Amidi has been elected as Iraq's new president, securing 227 out of 249 votes in parliament. His election follows a period of political maneuvering and marks a significant development in the country's leadership.INDIA

Defence Minister Rajnath Singh affirmed the government's unwavering commitment to implement the Women's Reservation Act, asserting that no power can prevent its enforcement despite past opposition. Prime Minister Narendra Modi also reassured that no state would face discrimination in the delimitation process linked to the women's quota law, emphasizing that the proportion of Lok Sabha seats for any state would not decrease.
  • 2. The death toll from a blast at the Vedanta power plant in Chhattisgarh's Sakti district has tragically risen to 24, with another worker succumbing to their injuries. Eleven other individuals continue to receive medical treatment in various hospitals following the incident.
  • 3. At least 14 workers lost their lives and six others were injured in a devastating explosion at a Vanaja fireworks unit near Virudhunagar district in Tamil Nadu. Rescue personnel struggled to extinguish the flames amidst continuous explosions of chemicals and unfinished goods, with officials investigating whether the unit had proper permits to operate on a Sunday.
  • 4. The Punjab Police announced the dismantling of a terror module reportedly backed by Pakistan's ISI, arresting one individual and recovering four hand grenades along with two foreign-made pistols and ammunition in a joint operation.
  • 5. The Opposition has declared its intention to boycott the election for the Deputy Chairman of the Rajya Sabha as a sign of protest against the Modi government's failure to appoint a Deputy Speaker in the Lok Sabha for the past seven years. Congress leader Jairam Ramesh stated that the government had not engaged in meaningful consultations on the matter.
