Share Market

Global financial markets are experiencing significant volatility as investors remain cautious amid the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict and instability surrounding key oil transit routes. The FTSE 100 closed down 0.75 percent to 10,379, and the FTSE 250 fell 0.8 percent to 22,582.81, reflecting broader market anxiety. Tech stocks, particularly the Magnificent Seven, are showing resilience as investors view them as a win-win trade that is more insulated from rising oil prices than other market sectors. The U.S. continues to strengthen its position as a major oil exporter as super-sized vessels turn west to load U.S. crude, aiming to capitalize on the supply crisis caused by the conflict. Agricultural sectors are closely monitoring rising fuel and fertilizer prices resulting from the disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. Investors are awaiting key economic data releases this week, including U.S. consumer confidence and building permits, to gauge the impact of current geopolitical tensions. Small-cap stocks on the London Stock Exchange's AIM market have shown volatile swings, with initial sell-offs followed by rapid, modest recoveries. The U.S. Treasury has sanctioned a shadow fleet of 19 vessels for transporting billions of dollars worth of Iranian energy products to foreign markets. India's private investment in the space sector has exceeded 600 million dollars over the past five years, showing strong growth despite broader regional economic instability. The Bank of England's independent role remains a topic of analysis as markets digest the ongoing geopolitical and economic pressures.
